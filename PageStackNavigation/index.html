About

Aurora, established in early 2015, is a group of students from Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, Delhi (IIITD) coming together with a common interest in research and with a zeal to develop innovative products, in the field of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs).
We identify ourselves as “Aurora”, symbolic of the majestic and transcending aurora lights of the Arctic Region and our institute IIITD. The team has been working with an aim to incorporate innovative technologies in the UAV’s, to bring multifunctional capabilities in it.
As part of the ‘Infosys Centre of Artificial Intelligence’ at IIIT-Delhi, Aurora aims to design industry-level UAVs which would solve real-life problems. The team comprises of a diverse group of students having expertise in computer vision, aeromodelling, computer networking, autonomous and embedded systems.


Vision

Human Detection

We started with the problem of human detection in aerial imagery. Though the final goal is to be able to do that from a height range at which a UAV flies ( 200 to 400 ft. ), because of lack of a proper dataset at the time, we started off with using the INRIA person dataset. An SVM was trained using HOG features extracted from this dataset. Then, we picked up a random video from YouTube and applied this detection strategy. Here is the link to the result.

Letter Recognition

Again, the final goal is to be able to detect and recognise letters in aerial imagery collected from a height of about 200 ft. to 400 ft. The dimension of letters being of about 8 ft. by 8 ft. To start off with the task we scaled down the whole problem and collected some data. Implementation of the detection and recognition strategy is underway.


Design

The Autopilot that we have chosen is PixHawk because it is small and light with low power consumption and no coiling requirements. We chose Skywalker because it’s cost is low and is capable of carrying multiple payloads with a low inversion. The low weight allows the plane to land on almost any surface without damaging the fuselage. A new camera is being used which would help in achieving the desired tasks easily and results in a better output. This year, more emphasis is being given to safety. All the safety measures are checked prior to any flight.
